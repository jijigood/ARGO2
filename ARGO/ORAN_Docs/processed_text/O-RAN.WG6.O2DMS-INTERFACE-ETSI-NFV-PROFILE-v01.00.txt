Chapter 2 Profiling concepts  179 
2.1 Introduction 180 
The O-RAN framework follows similar principles as other frameworks and solutions related to network functions 181 
virtualisation and cloudification that have been or are being specified in other organizations, such as in the ETSI NFV. 182 
O-RAN.WG6.ORCH-USE-CASES [2] documents a set of use cases regarding the deployment of O-RAN NF on O-183 
Clouds, as well as it specifies relevant functional and interface requirements between the SMO and O-Cloud. O-184 
RAN.WG6.CAD [3] documents the cloudification deployment aspects of O-RAN NF based on VMs, OS container in 185 
VMs, or OS containers. These same forms of NF deployment are also considered by the ETSI NFV framework. 186 
With the objective to reuse existing network operator assets, e.g., as presently in use in other network deployments 187 
beyond RAN, the present document aims at specifying a profile of the ETSI NFV specifications that can also be used 188 
for the deployment of O-RAN NF on O-Clouds. 189 
The present O-Cloud O2dms profile based on ETSI NFV demarcates a defined protocol and data model between the 190 
SMO and O-Cloud based on the standardized virtualised resource, containerized workload, and VNF management 191 
capabilities of the functional entities and functions of the NFV-MANO architectural framework summarized in clause 192 
2.2. 193 
In the definition of the applicable protocol and data models to profile, two main demarcations can be defined depending 194 
on the end point of interaction of the SMO towards the O-Cloud: 195 
- "O2-D demarcation #1": SMO interacts with an O-Cloud DMS that offer VNFM-like functionality. This case 196 
considers a more abstract "NF Deployment" view, i.e., O-Cloud DMS supports deployment of defined O-RAN 197 
NFs. The "NF Deployment" view is also referred in numerous use cases of O-RAN-WG6.ORCH-USE-CASES 198 
[2]. The "NF Deployment" concept is defined in the O-RAN.WG6.O2-GA&P [4] and O-RAN.WG6.CAD [3]. 199 
Clause 2.2.1 of the same O-RAN.WG6.O2-GA&P [4] describes the DMS as the functional block responsible for 200 
the management of NF Deployments into the O-Cloud. 201 
- "O2-D demarcation #2": SMO interacts with an O-Cloud DMS that offers virtualised resource/containerized 202 
workload management functionality; thus, SMO performs VNFM-like functionality. This case considers a more 203 
granular "virtualised resource/containerized workload" management view, i.e., O-Cloud DMS has no notion at 204 
all of O-RAN NFs. 205 
These two demarcation cases are depicted in figures 2.1-1 and 2.1-2, respectively. 206 
NOTE 1: VIM/CISM and NFVI/CIS placement in the architecture figures are not meant to be accurate and it is not 207 
the focus of these figures to present the architectural mappings of other elements such as SMO, NFVO, 208 
etc. Purpose of the figures is to illustrate the demarcations/boundaries of applicability for the O2dms 209 
ETSI NFV profiles. 210 
NOTE 2: More information about the NFV-MANO architectural framework is provided in clause 2.2. 211

2.2 ETSI NFV framework and solutions 215 
ETSI NFV defines the NFV-MANO architectural framework, which is conformed of a set of functional blocks, 216 
functions and interfaces enabling the deployment and management of VM and container-based VNFs. The management 217 
of virtualised and container-based resources is performed by technology and solution agnostic functional entities 218 
according to a specified set of interfaces, models and APIs. 219 
The list of NFV-MANO architectural framework functional blocks and functions and their main responsibilities are: 220 
- Virtualised Infrastructure Manager (VIM): responsible for the management of virtualised resources (virtual 221 
compute, virtual network and virtual storage) in the NFV Infrastructure (NFVI) and VM software images, 222 
- Container Infrastructure Service Management (CISM): responsible for the management of container-based 223 
resources and container workloads in Container Infrastructure Service (CIS) clusters,  224 
- Container Image Registry (CIR): responsible for the management of OS container software images, 225 
- VNF Manager (VNFM): responsible for the management of VNFs, including their lifecycle, and provides 226 
corresponding management services based on a common set of interfaces and models regardless of the 227 
technology used for implementing the VNF, be it container or VM-based, and 228 
- NFV Orchestrator (NFVO): responsible for the management of VNF Packages and other artifacts and the overall 229 
orchestration of NFVI resources across multiple sites (referred as well as NFVI-PoPs) and the lifecycle 230 
management of Network Services (NS). 231 
ETSI GS NFV-IFA 010 [8] specifies the principles and scope of the NFV-MANO architectural framework and 232 
functional requirements of the functional blocks and functions that are part of it. 233 
A set of groups specifications (GS) specifies the interfaces exposed and consumed by the NFV-MANO functional 234 
blocks and functions. The following are the GS of relevance for the present document: 235 
- ETSI GS NFV-IFA 007 [7] specifies stage 2 interfaces and associated information model of interfaces between 236 
the NFVO and the VNFM. ETSI GS NFV-SOL 003 [11] specifies stage 3 protocols and data models of these 237 
interfaces based on RESTful. The Release 4 version of ETSI GS NFV-SOL 003 [28] is expected to add support 238 
for container-based VNF deployments as documented in the ongoing publicly available draft. Refer to clause 239

In addition to the composition of the NFV-MANO architectural framework in terms of functional blocks, functions and 255 
interfaces, one of the key aspects is the definition of both design-time and runtime elements. Design-time elements 256 
basically comprise those objects and artifacts that describe the design and requirements for the deployment of entities 257 
into or using the framework. The design-time objects comprise packages and descriptors, among which the relevant 258 
ones for the present document are the VNF Package and VNF Descriptor (VNFD). 259 
- ETSI GS NFV-IFA 011 [12] specifies stage 2 requirements related to VNF Package and VNF metadata and the 260 
information model of the VNFD. ETSI GS NFV-SOL 001 [10] specifies stage 3 data model of the VNFD (in 261 
addition to other descriptors such as Network Service Descriptor (NSD) and Physical Network Function 262 
Descriptor (PNFD)) based on TOSCA, while ETSI GS NFV-SOL 006 [14] specifies the same elements but 263 
based on YANG. ETSI GS NFV-SOL 004 [13] specifies the data model and structure of the VNF Packages. 264 
2.3 ETSI NFV profiling for O2dms interfaces 265 
2.3.1 General 266 
The present and subsequent clauses describe specific aspects of the ETSI NFV profiling for the O2dms interfaces. In the 267 
present profiling version based on the "O2-D demarcation #1" introduced in clause 2.1, the functional capabilities of the 268 
NFVO, VNFM, VIM and CISM of the NFV-MANO architectural framework are mapped to the O-RAN framework as 269 
defined in clause 2.3.2. 270 
The O2dms ETSI NFV profile based on "O2-D demarcation #1" is based on the ETSI GS NFV-SOL 003 [11]. The 271 
ETSI GS NFV-SOL 003 [11] specifies, among others, the RESTful API of the "VNF Lifecycle Management interface", 272 
which is used as the foundation for providing an O-Cloud DMS exposed API for the lifecycle management of NF 273 
Deployments of O-RAN. Specific API correspondences are defined in clause 2.3.3. 274 
Finally, for the orchestration and management operations through the O2dms ETSI NFV profile, it is assumed that 275 
descriptors and packaging of corresponding NF Deployments, and consequently of O-RAN NF, are on-boarded and 276 
available to the SMO and O-Cloud DMS. In particular, it is expected that the descriptors and packaging for O-RAN NF 277 
contain relevant metadata such as identifiers of the design-time definitions of components/constituents of an NF 278 
Deployment, which are also information either conveyed or exposed through the API. Additional mapping information 279 
about descriptor and packaging artifacts is provided in clause 2.3.2, and reference to specific elements concerning 280 
various orchestration aspects are further described in clause 2.5. 281

2.5.3 Network connectivity for NF Deployments 708 
Network connectivity information of NF Deployments in the O2dms ETSI NFV profile is exposed and/or conveyed via 709 
specific data types. Furthermore, in terms of network connectivity the profile modelling is based on the following key 710 
concepts: virtual links and connection points. 711 
A virtual link is an abstraction of a network, which is used by the management and orchestration functions in the ETSI 712 
NFV architectural framework. Once applied to the O-RAN framework, virtual links also become an abstraction of 713 
connectivity which is exposed and used over the O2dms interface. Virtual links can be realized in various forms such as 714 
virtual/overlay networks in an O-Cloud site, or a composition of various networks. Virtual links can be internal or 715 
external to an NF Deployment: 716 
- Internal virtual link: it expresses the connectivity between the components or constituents of an NF Deployment. 717 
- External virtual link: it expresses the connectivity external to the NF Deployment, i.e., networks that are used to 718 
interconnect among NF Deployments, or between NF Deployments and other entities such as to other O-RAN 719 
NF, xApps, or even other systems such as management and orchestration ones. 720 
Internal virtual links can be modelled in two forms depending on the entity that manages the resources realizing the 721 
virtual link: 722 
- NF Deployment virtual link: it denotes an internal NF Deployment virtual link that is managed by the O-Cloud 723 
DMS. The data type providing information about such kind of virtual link is named 724 
"VnfVirtualLinkResourceInfo" (see clause 3.2.6.3.9). 725 
- Externally-managed virtual link: it denotes an internal NF Deployment virtual link that is managed externally to 726 
the O-Cloud DMS, e.g., in the present profiling case the SMO. The data type providing information about such 727 
kind of virtual link is named "ExtManagedVirtualLinkInfo" (see clause 3.2.6.3.7). 728 
NOTE 1: The reason for differentiating the two types comes from the fact that there are deployment cases where 729 
the network resources are managed by organizations or systems different to those managing the NF 730 
Deployments. 731 
A connection point is also an abstraction that denotes the port/interface of connectivity of an NF Deployment or a 732 
constituent within an NF Deployment to a network. Similarly, as with the virtual link case, connections points are 733 
further qualified as internal or external connections points of an NF Deployment. 734 
- Internal connections points: these are the connection points used to connect to the internal virtual links of an NF 735 
Deployment. The structured "vnfcCpInfo" attribute in the "VnfcResourceInfo" provides information about such 736 
kind of connection point (see clause 3.2.6.3.8). 737 
- External connection points: these are the connection points used to connect the NF Deployment to the external 738 
virtual links. The data type providing information about such kind of connection point is named "VnfExtCpInfo" 739 
(see clause 3.2.6.3.4). 740 
External connection points can be associated to various types of other connection points. For instance, an external 741 
connection point can be realized by an actual internal connection point of an NF Deployment, i.e., the internal 742 
connection point is re-exposed externally and used for connecting to the external virtual link. This is denoted by a series 743 
of specific attributes in the "VnfExtCpInfo". 744 
In addition, specific connection points are also defined depending on how they are further realized or mapped to. 745 
- Virtual IP connection point: it denotes a connection point which is basically a Virtual IP (VIP). 746 
- Virtual connection point: it denotes a connection point which does not have a specific virtual resource 747 
representation in the infrastructure; instead, it is typically realized by some form of configuration into one or 748 
multiple resources. 749 
Depending on the type of connection point, additional elements are modelled, such as the virtual link ports and network 750 
attachment definition resources. 751
