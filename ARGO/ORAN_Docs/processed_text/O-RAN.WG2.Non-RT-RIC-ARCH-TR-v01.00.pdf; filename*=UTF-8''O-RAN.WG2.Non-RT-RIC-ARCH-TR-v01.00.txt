4 Overview of Non-RT RIC20
4.1 Non-RT RIC in O-RAN Overall Architecture21
In the O-RAN Logical Architecture, as seen in Figure 4.1-1 below, the Non-RT RIC is portrayed as a function that22
resides within the Service Management and Orchestration Framework and which has a direct association with the A123
interface. The Non-RT RIC’s position internal to, as opposed to interfacing with, the SMO is intended to communicate24
thattheNon-RTRICiscomprisedofasubsetoffunctionalityoftheSMOitself.25
The direct association with the A1 interface is intended to convey that the functionality of the Non-RT RIC is directly26
responsible for driving that which is sent and received across the A1 interface. The O1 and O2 interfaces are shown as27
being directly associated with the SMO Framework itself, that which is not specific to the Non-RT RIC. This28
representation is intended to communicate that the SMO Framework functionality, but not the Non-RT RIC29
functionalitytherein,isdirectlyresponsiblefordrivingthatwhichiscarriedacrosstheseinterfaces.30
The representation of the Non-RT RIC in the center (as opposed to the bordering the bottom edge) of the SMO31
Framework visibly indicates that the Non-RT RIC can access SMO Framework functionality, including influencing that32
which is carriedover the O1interface. Whileitisnot prohibitedfrom accessing any SMO Framework functionality,the33
Non-RT RIC’s role of RAN resource optimization per current O-RAN definition implies that the Non-RT RIC would34
only access SMO Framework functionality for that purpose. Thus, the Non-RT RIC would influence that which is35
carriedacrosstheO2interfaceonlytotheextentthattheO-CloudisconsideredaRANresource.36
