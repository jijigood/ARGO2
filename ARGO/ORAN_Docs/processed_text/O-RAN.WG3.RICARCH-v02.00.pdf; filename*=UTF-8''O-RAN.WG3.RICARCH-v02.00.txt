6.2.3 Conflict Mitigation 4
In the context of Near-RT RIC, Conflict Mitigation is about addressing conflicting interactions between different xApps.  5
An application will typically change one or more parameters with the objective of optimizing a specific metric.  Conflict 6
Mitigation is necessary because xApps objectives may be chosen/configured such that they result in conflicting actions.   7
The control target of the radio resource management can be a cell, a UE or a bearer, etc. The control contents of the radio 8
resource management can cover access control, bearer control, hando ver control, QoS control, resource assignment and 9
so on. The control time span indicates the valid control duration which is expected by the control request. The conflicts 10
of control can be illustrated as below. 11
1) Direct Conflicts: The conflicts can be observed directly by Conflict Mitigation. Some cases are described as below:  12
- Two or more xApps request different settings for the very same configuration of one or more parameters of a  13
Control Target. Conflict mitigation processes the requests and decides on a resolution. 14
- The new request from an xApp may conflict with the running configuration resulting from a previous request 15
of another or the same xApp. 16
- The total requested resources from different xApps may exceed the limitation of the RAN system, e.g. the sum 17
of resources required by the two different xApps may be far beyond the resource limitation of the RAN system.  18
2) Indirect Conflicts: The conflicts cannot be observed directly, nevertheless, some dependence among the parameters 19
and resources that the xApps target can be observed. Conflict Mitigation may anticipate the possible conflicts and 20
take actions to mitigate them.  For instance , different xApps target different configuration parameters to optimize 21
the same metric accor ding to the respective objective. Even though this will not result in conflicting parameter 22
settings, it may have uncontrollable or inadvertent system impacts . One example of such indirect conflicts can 23
occur when the changes required by one xApp create a system impact which is equivalent to a parameter change 24
targeted by another xApp. E.g., antenna tilts and m easurement offsets are different control points, but they both 25
impact the handover boundary. 26
3) Implicit Conflicts: The conflicts cannot be observed directly, even the dependence between xApps are not obvious. 27
For instance, different xApps may optimize different metrics and (re-)configure different parameters. Nonetheless, 28
optimizing one metric may have implicit, unwanted, and maybe adversary side effects  on one of the metrics 29
optimized by another xApp. E.g., protecting throughput metrics for GBR users may degrade non GBR metrics or 30
even cell throughput. 31
For mitigating these conflicts, different approaches exist: 32
1) Direct conflicts typically can be mitigated by pre -action coordination, i.e., the xApps or a Conflict Mitigation 33
component needs to make the final determination on whether any specific change is made , or in which order the 34
changes are applied. 35
2) Indirect conflicts can be resolved by post-action verification. Here, the actions are executed and the effects on the 36
target metric are observed. Based on the observations, the system has to decide on potential corrections, e.g., 37
rolling back one of the xApp actions. 38
3) Implicit conflicts are the most difficult to mitigate since these dependencies are difficult or impossible to observe 39
and therefore hard to model in any mitigation scheme. In some cases, it may be possible to design around such 40
conflicts by ensuring that use cases (xApps) target different parameters, thus falling back to approach 2), but 41
preferably, a generic approach to managing such conflicts is established. 42

9.2.2 RIC functional procedures 16
9.2.2.1 E2 Subscription API procedure 17
The purpose of the E2 Subscription API procedure in the Near-RT RIC is to ensure that only validated and non-duplicate 18
RIC Subscription Request messages are issued by the Near -RT RIC over the E2 interface to  the E2 Node and that 19
duplicated E2 Subscription Request messages from xApps are handled gracefully. 20
This procedure is based on the following assumptions: 21
- xApp may obtain guidance from Near -RT RIC Platform (i.e. Conflict Mitigation) to resolve potential co nflicts 22
and/or detect partial duplications prior to sending a E2 Subscription Request (see section 9.2.3.1)  23
- xApp has been configured with a trusted xApp ID 24
- E2 related API routes E2 Subscription Request messages for one or more E2 nodes from xApp towards an 25
appropriate Near-RT RIC platform xApp Subscription Management function instance 26
- xApp Subscription Management may recover from a platform database the list of all previous successful RIC 27
