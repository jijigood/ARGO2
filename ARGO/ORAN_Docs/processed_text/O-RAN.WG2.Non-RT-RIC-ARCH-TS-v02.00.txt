 Non-RT RIC in O-RAN Overall Architecture 5 Non-Real Time RAN Intelligent Controller (Non-RT RIC) is the functionality internal to the Service Management and 6 Orchestration (SMO) framework in O-RAN overall architecture, as shown in Figure 4.1-2 of [2].  7  Non-RT RIC represents a subset of functionality of the SMO framework. 8  Non-RT RIC supports intelligent RAN operation and optimization.  9  Non-RT RIC logically terminates the A1 interface, and it provides policy-based guidance, enrichment 10 information, and AI/ML model management [FFS] to the Near-RT RICs.  11  Non-RT RIC can access other SMO framework functionalities, for example influencing what is carried across 12 the O1 and O2 interface. 13 
 Non-RT RIC Composition 14 Non-RT RIC is comprised of: 15  Non-RT RIC Framework – Functionality internal to the SMO Framework that: 16 o Logically terminates the A1 interface to the Near-RT RIC. 17 o Exposes set of R1 services to Non-RT RIC Applications (rApps). 18  Non-RT RIC Applications (rApps) – Applications that leverage the functionalities available in the Non-RT RIC 19 Framework / SMO Framework to provide value added services related to RAN operation and optimization. The 20 scope of rApps includes, but is not limited to, radio resource management, data analytics, and providing 21 enrichment information. 22 3 Requirements 23

 General Description 5 A service is a set of capabilities related to each other, offered by a service producer to service consumer(s) for 6 consumption. The capabilities within a service typically have a common purpose and can be inter-dependent. In order to 7 improve re-usability, the mutual coupling across any two services is usually avoided or minimized. Services are produced 8 by service producers and consumed by service consumers through service endpoints (typically APIs). The interoperability 9 of service producers and service consumers is ensured by support of the related service endpoints, but it does not depend 10 on the decomposition of logical functions. Being a service producer and/or a service consumer is a role of an rApp or of 11 a logical function in the SMO or the Non-RT RIC framework (see Clause 4). 12 The set of R1 services which can be accessed via the R1 interface includes (but is not limited to): 13  Service management and exposure services, which include service registration and service discovery services, 14 authentication services, authorization services and communication support services. The service management 15 and exposure services support extensibility of the set of R1 services. 16  Data management and exposure services, which deliver data created or collected by Data Producers to Data 17 Consumers according to their needs. 18  A1-related services, which provide access to functionality related to A1. 19  AI/ML workflow services, which provide access to AI and ML workflow. 20  O1-related services, which provide access to functionality related to O1. 21  O2-related services, which provide access to functionality related to O2. 22 NOTE: A part of the services listed above may be used on other O-RAN-defined interfaces as well. 23

 Service management and exposure services 26 The following service management and exposure services are defined: 27  Bootstrap (Optional): It enables an rApp to discover the endpoints of the service management and exposure 28 service.  29  Services registration: It allows to register services which includes storing and maintaining profile about each 30 service.  31  Services deregistration: It allows to deregister services and delete the service profile. 32  Service discovery: It supports service discovery for a service consumer to find services based on its selection 33 criteria if it is provided.  34

 Data management and exposure services 9 The following data management and exposure services are defined:  10  Data registration: It allows Data Producers to communicate information about the data types that they are able 11 to produce. 12  Data discovery: It allows Data Consumers to discover available data types in the SMO/Non-RT RIC framework. 13 Data Consumers need to be authorized to discover the data types.  14  Data subscription: It allows Data Consumers to use subscribe/notify procedure to obtain data for consumption.  15  Data request: It allows Data Consumers to use request/response procedure to obtain data for consumption. 16  Collection of data: It allows the SMO/Non-RT RIC framework to collect produced data from Data Producers. 17  Delivery of data: It allows to deliver data from the SMO/Non-RT RIC framework to Data Consumers for 18 consumption.  19  Data processing [Optional]: It allows service consumers to access data processing in the SMO/Non-RT RIC 20 framework. 21 Data Producers (e.g., rApps) communicate information about the data types that they produce in data registration. Data 22 Producers can communicate constraints related to the authorization of the Data Consumers to discover registered data 23 types and access produced data. Data Producers communicate information about how the data are collected by the 24 SMO/Non-RT RIC framework (e.g., collection periodicity, event-trigger conditions, push or pull, etc.). 25 Data Consumers (e.g., rApps) communicate information about the data types that they want to discover. Data Consumers 26 subscribe/request data from the SMO/Non-RT RIC framework for consumption with specific data type, periodicity, and 27 delivery (or reporting) method, scope, etc. The delivery (or reporting) method contains information about how the 28 subscribed/requested data are delivered from the SMO/Non-RT RIC framework (e.g., delivery periodicity, event-trigger 29 conditions, push or pull, etc.). 30 SMO/Non-RT RIC framework collects data produced by Data Producers. SMO/Non-RT RIC framework delivers data to 31 Data Consumers for consumption. SMO/Non-RT RIC framework can optionally store collected data. SMO/Non-RT RIC 32 framework can optionally process collected and/or stored data (e.g., quantization, normalization, labelling, correlation, 33 etc.).  34
