3 Definition of terms, symbols and abbreviations 16
3.1 Terms 17
For the purposes of the present document, the terms and definitions given in [2] and the following apply. A term defined 18
in the present document takes precedence over the definition of the same term, if any, in [2]. For the base station classes 19
of Pico, Micro and Macro, the definitions are given in [3]. 20
Carrier Frequency:  Center frequency of the cell.  21
F1 interface:  The standard interface between an O-CU and an O-DUx specified in [8]. 22
Integrated architecture:  An architecture wherein the O-RUx and O-DUx are implemented on one platform. Each O -23
RUx and RF frontend are associated with one O-DUx and are connected to O-CU via F1 interface 24
Split architecture:  An architecture wherein the O-RUx and O-DUx are physically separated from one another. A switch 25
may aggregate multiple O-RUx (s) to one O-DUx.  The O-DUx, switch and O-RUx (s) are connected by the fronthaul 26
interface as defined in [4]. 27
Transmission Reception Point (TRxP ):  Antenna array with one or more antenna elements available to the network 28
located at a specific geographical location for a specific area. 29
Occupied Bandwidth (OBW): It refers to the bandwidth occupied by the base station when operated, defined by the sum 30
of the active bandwidths of the band allocation(s) operated . As defined by 3GPP TS 34.121 section 5.8 , occupied 31
bandwidth is the bandwidth containing 99% of the total integrated power  of the transmitted spectrum, centered on the 32
assigned channel frequency. The bandwidth between the 0.5% power frequency points is the occupied bandwidth . 33
Instantaneous Bandwidth (IBW): It refers to the bandwidth in which all frequency components can be si multaneously 34
analyzed. It is defined by the frequency boundaries of the operating band(s). 35
Frequency Range: It refers to bandwidth defined by the frequency range within which the Base Station can be operated, 36
defined by the band-pass filter of the BS; e.g., 3.4 – 3.8 GHz (400 MHz) 37
gNB: A RAN node providing NR user plane and control plane protocol terminations towards the UE, and connected 38
via the NG interface to the 5GC 39

Frequency Band: A designated frequency range for the operation of the base station and the UE radios. 5G 1
NR frequency bands are divided into two different frequency ranges: Frequency Range 1 (FR1) that mainly includes 2

5 White Box Hardware Architecture 1
Based on the gNB physical implementation architectures discussed earlier, this chapter provides the architecture, major 2
building blocks and all external/internal interfaces for each whitebox.    3
5.1 O-CU Hardware Architecture 4
In 3GPP system architecture, the gNB Central Unit (CU) communicates with the Distributed Unit (DU) via an F1 interface 5
[8].  This interface has been adopted by O -RAN Alliance for O-CU and O-DU connection.  F1 is an IP -based protocol 6
interface. F1 interface is split into F1-C and F1-U interfaces.  7
F1-C interface is used for the management of interface setup/configuration, system information, UE context, RRC 8
transfer, paging and warning message transfer. F1-U interface is used for user data transfer and flow control.  9

accelerator block.  The O -CU functions can  be implemented either on separated hardware platform s or on the same 12
hardware platforms integrated with O-DUx functions. In either case, the O-CU hosts L2/L3 functions, where as O-DU 13
hosts L2/L1 functions which require different CPU, Storage, Acceleration and Networking capabilities. Figure 5.1-1 14
shows the hardware blocks and interfaces within the O-CU white box. 15

5.13  mMIMO O-RU7-2x Functional Module Description 3
In this section description and diagram for three types of possible NxM mMIMO O-RU High Level System Architecture 4
are provided. mMIMO O-RU7-2x can be realized with different high level radio architecture based on various functional 5

The level of integration achieved on an SoC for different functional blocks can vary and that in turn can result in different 7
O-RU architecture and the associated interconnects between the functional blocks.  8
These functional splits can be realized through three possible configuration O -RU Architecture options as presented in 9
Figures 5.13-1, 5.13-2, and 5.13-3.    10
In mMIMO O-RU High Level System Architecture #1 as shown in Figure 5.13-1, eCPRI frame that comes from O-DU7-11
2x is compressed/decompressed and processed by O-RAN Fronthaul Processing Unit & Beamformer block. Additionally, 12
security compliance may be carried out according to WG11 specifications. Subsequently, Digital Processing Unit that 13
have FPGA/ASIC based DFE module receives the packets from O-RAN Fronthaul Processing Unit & Beamformer block 14
and performs the functionalities such as compression/decompression, Low-Phy, DDC, DUC, CFR, and DPD. The ADC 15
and DAC functions are performed in AFE block. All these three functional blocks can be part of a single SoC or can be 16
split into multiple physical integrated circuits (IC) and connected with digital or packet-based interface. 17
The functional block labelled RF Section, consists of an optional frequency converter (mixer), Power Amplifier (PA)/ 18
Low Noise Amplifier (LNA). The filter is the next functional block in the pat h and is needed for each of the N Tx/Rx 19
paths to meet the emissions in DL and provide the necessary selectivity for UL. The last functional block in the mMIMO 20
RU system is the Antenna. Depending on the power level for each of the Tx chain in a mMIMO system , the filter could 21
be integrated into the RF Section or could be integrated along with the antenna thus making it the AFU.   22
In mMIMO O -RU, Tx/Rx reciprocity calibration is one of the important steps for optimal performance. Calibration is 23
required to align the Tx and Rx chains in phase and amplitude. In some implementations, reciprocity calibration requires 24
to implement a dedicated feedback path. Multiple options are possible for the design of such feedback path – the main 25
three options are shown in Figures 5.13-1, 5.13-2, and 5.13-3. The main difference between each of the calibration options 26
is the implementation of the feedback network and where the RF feedback and associated distribution circuitry resides. 27
