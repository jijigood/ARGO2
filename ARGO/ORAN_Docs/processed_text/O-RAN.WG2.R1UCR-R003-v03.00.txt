For the purposes of the present document, the following terms apply: 1 
Non-RT RIC: O-RAN non-real-time RAN Intelligent Controller: a logical function in the SMO framework that 2 
enables non-real-time control and optimization of RAN elements and resources, AI/ML workflow including 3 
model training and updates, and policy-based guidance of applications/features in Near-RT RIC. The Non-RT 4 
RIC is comprised of the Non-RT RIC framework and Non-RT RIC applications (rApps). 5 
Non-RT RIC framework: Functionality internal to the SMO framework that logically terminates the A1 6 
interface and provides the R1services to rApps through the R1 interface. 7 
rApp: Non-RT RIC application: an application designed to consume and/or produce R1 Services.  8 
 NOTE:  rApps can leverage the functionality provided by the SMO/Non-RT RIC framework to deliver 9 
value added services related to intelligent RAN optimization and operation. 10

6 Use cases for Service Management and Exposure 4 
6.1 SME use case 1: Registration and Bootstrap  5 
6.1.1 Overview 6 
This use case provides the description and requirements for registering an rApp with the SMO/Non-RT RIC 7 
framework. 8 
6.2.1 Background and goal of the use case 9 
The rApp registration procedure registers the rApp to the SMO/Non-RT RIC framework. It may use the 10 
bootstrap service to discover the rApp registration API. To register an rApp, an rApp may pass relevant info 11 
that may include the rApp name, vendor, software version, and other information needed by the 12 
SMO/Non-RT RIC framework.  13 
6.3.1 Entities/resources involved in the use case 14 
1) SME functions in the role of SME services Producer  15 
a. support functionality that allows the rApp to retrieve the bootstrap information that enables it 16 
to discover the SME services, 17 
b. support functionality to authenticate the rApp,  18 
c. assigns the rApp with an ID.  19 
2) rApp  20 
a. retrieves the bootstrap information that enables it to discover the SME services, 21 
b. initiates the procedure of rApp registration. 22

6.2 SME Use case 2: Manage Service Registration 1 
6.2.1 Overview 2 
This use case allows an rApp as Service Producer to register each of its produced services, update the 3 
service information of each of its registered services and deregister each of its registered service as 4 
specified in R1GAP[[1]]. 5 
6.2.2 Background and goal of the use case 6 
An rApp as a Service Producer can register each of the new services it producers. Other rApps that are 7 
Service Consumers can discover the registered services. 8 
After an rApp as a Service Producer has successfully registered the each of the services it produces, it can 9 
update and/or deregister each of the registered services. 10 
6.2.3 Entities/resources involved in the use case 11 
1) SME functions in the role of SME services Producer  12 
a. support functionality to register a service produced by an rApp,   13 
b. support authorization of an rApp to determine whether an rApp can register a service, update 14 
a registered service, and deregister a registered service, 15 
c. support validation of the new service produced by an rApp that includes determining whether 16 
there are conflicts with services registered earlier, 17 
d. provide the response of success or failure result to Register service request, Update service 18 
registration request and Deregister service request. 19 
2) rApp  20 
a. supports to initiate the procedure to register a service it produces, update a registered service, 21 
and deregister a registered service. 22

7.3 DME use case 3: Data offer 1 
7.3.1 Overview 2 
This use case allows a Data Producer rApp as Service Consumer to create and terminate a data offer to 3 
trigger the collection of a data instance it wishes to deliver to the Data management and exposure 4 
functions as Data management and exposure services Producer for collection. 5 
Further, it allows the Data management and exposure functions as Data management and exposure 6 
services Producer to indicate to the Data Producer rApp as Service Consumer that they intend to stop 7 
collecting the data instance related to a data offer. 8 
7.3.2 Background and goal of the use case 9 
A Data Producer rApp as Service Consumer can create a data offer which indicates to the Data 10 
management and exposure functions that the rApp intends to deliver a data instance to the Data 11 
management and exposure services Producer for collection.  12 
Further, the Data Producer rApp as Service Consumer can terminate a data offer it has created earlier to 13 
indicate to the Data management and exposure functions that the Data Producer no longer wishes to 14 
deliver the data instance for collection. 15 
Finally, the Data management and exposure functions can indicate to the Data Producer rApp as Service 16 
Consumer via a data offer termination notification that they will stop collecting a data instance and will 17 
terminate the related data offer created earlier by the Data Producer rApp. 18 
7.3.3 Entities/resources involved in the use case 19 
1) Data management and exposure functions as Data management and exposure services Producer  20 
a. support functionality to create a data offer as requested by a Data Producer rApp,   21 
b. support authorization of a Data Producer rApp to determine whether it can create a data offer, 22 
c. support to send data offer termination notifications, 23 
d. support to receive the offered data from the rApp and store them, 24 
e. provide the response of success or failure result to the create data offer request and the 25 
terminate data offer request. 26 
2) rApp  27 
a. supports initiating the procedure to create a data offer indicating its intent to deliver data for 28 
collection and subsequent storage,  29 
b. supports to receive data offer termination notifications, 30 
c. supports to request terminating a data offer created earlier, 31 
d. supports to produce and deliver the offered data.  32
