4.2.2 Test configuration 5
The test configuration for E2 conformance testing of Near-RT RIC is illustrated in Figure 4.2.2-1 which shows a test 6
setup that employs an E2 Node simulator, O-RU simulator, and UE simulator as a simplification.  A test set-up without 7
use of simulations would require inclusion of an O-RU with associated M-Plane support and also a real UE or test UE. 8
 9
In as much as the conformance testing involves configuring the E2 Nodes and the Near- RT RIC, it shall be performed 10
by means of the O1 interface. Configuration of the E2 Nodes and Near-RT RIC to operate in nominal state via the O1 11
interface and configuring for policy guidance and enrichment data to the Near -RT RIC via the A1 interface are outside  12
the scope of this test specification.  13

entities, the E2 Node simulator may support a range of different RAN configurations similar to the list of cases 19
described in “O-RAN.WG1.O-RAN-Architecture-Description” [8], Annex A.4.  Examples include: 20
- Standalone O-CU-CP connected to one or more standalone O-CU-UP and one or more standalone O-DU.   Each 21
logical node is considered as an E2 Node that presents an E2 interface to the Near- RT RIC. 22
- Combined O-CU-CP and O-CU-UP connected to one or more standalone O-DU. The combined O-CU-CP/O-23
CU-UP may present either a common E2 interface or individual E2 interfaces corresponding to the individual O -24
RAN components 25
- Combined O-CU-CP, O-CU-UP and O-DU.  The combined node may present either a common E2 interface or 26
individual E2 interfaces corresponding to the individual O-RAN components 27

4.2.2.1 Device Under Test (Near-RT RIC) 8
