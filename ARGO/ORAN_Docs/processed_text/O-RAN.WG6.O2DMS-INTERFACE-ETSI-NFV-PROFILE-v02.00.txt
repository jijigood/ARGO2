Chapter 2  Profiling concepts  167 
2.1 Introduction 168 
The O-RAN framework follows similar principles as other frameworks and solutions related to network functions 169 
virtualisation and cloudification that have been or are being specified in other organizations, such as in the ETSI NFV. 170 
O-RAN.WG6.ORCH-USE-CASES [2] documents a set of use cases regarding the deployment of O-RAN NF on O-171 
Clouds, as well as it specifies relevant functional and interface requirements betwee n the SMO and O-Cloud. O-172 
RAN.WG6.CAD [3] documents the cloudification deployment aspects of O-RAN NF based on VMs, OS container in 173 
VMs, or OS containers. These same forms of NF deployment are also considered by the ETSI NFV framework.  174 
With the objective to reuse existing network operator assets, e.g., as presently in use in other network deployments 175 
beyond RAN, the present document aims at specifying a profile of the ETSI NFV specifications that can also be used 176 
for the deployment of O-RAN NF on O-Clouds. 177 
The present O-Cloud O2dms profile based on ETSI NFV demarcates a defined protocol and data model between the 178 
SMO and O-Cloud based on the standardized virtualised resource, containerized workload, and VNF management 179 
capabilities of the functional entities and functions of the NFV-MANO architectural framework summarized in clause 180 
2.2. 181 
In the definition of the applicable protocol and data models to profile, two main demarcations can be defined depending 182 
on the end point of interaction of the SMO towards the O-Cloud: 183 
- "O2-D demarcation #1": SMO interacts with an O-Cloud DMS that offer VNFM-like functionality. This case 184 
considers a more abstract "NF Deployment" view, i.e., O-Cloud DMS supports deployment of defined O-RAN 185 
NFs. The "NF Deployment" view is also referred in numerous use cases of O -RAN-WG6.ORCH-USE-CASES 186 
[2]. The "NF Deployment" concept is defined in the O-RAN.WG6.O2-GA&P [4] and O-RAN.WG6.CAD [3]. 187 
Clause 2.2.1 of the same O-RAN.WG6.O2-GA&P [4] describes the DMS as the functional block responsible for 188 
the management of NF Deployments into the O-Cloud. 189 
- "O2-D demarcation #2": SMO interacts with an O-Cloud DMS that offers virtualised resource/containerized 190 
workload management functionality; thus, SMO performs VNFM-like functionality. This case considers a more 191 
granular "virtualised resource/containerized workload" management view, i.e., O -Cloud DMS has no notion at 192 
all of O-RAN NFs. 193 
These two demarcation cases are depicted in figures 2.1-1 and 2.1-2, respectively. 194

etc. Purpose of the figures is to illustrate the demarcations/boundaries of applicability for the O2dms 197 
ETSI NFV profiles. 198

2.2 ETSI NFV framework and solutions 203 
ETSI NFV defines the NFV-MANO architectural framework, which is conformed of a set of functional blocks, 204 
functions and interfaces enabling the deployment and management of VM and container-based VNFs. The management 205 
of virtualised and container-based resources is performed by technology and solution agnostic functional entities 206 
according to a specified set of interfaces, models and APIs. 207 
The list of NFV-MANO architectural framework functional blocks and functions and their main responsibilities are:  208 
- Virtualised Infrastructure Manager (VIM): responsible for the management of virtualised resources (virtual 209 
compute, virtual network and virtual storage) in the NFV Infrastructure (NFVI) and VM software images, 210 
- Container Infrastructure Service Management (CISM): responsible for the management of container -based 211 
resources and container workloads in Container Infrastructure Service (CIS) clusters,  212 
- Container Image Registry (CIR): responsible for the management of OS container software images, 213 
- VNF Manager (VNFM): responsible for the management of VNFs, including their lifecycle, and provides 214 
corresponding management services based on a common set of interfaces and models regardless of the 215 
technology used for implementing the VNF, be it container or VM-based, and 216 
- NFV Orchestrator (NFVO): responsible for the management of VNF Packages and other artifacts and the overall 217 
orchestration of NFVI resources across multiple sites (referred as well as NFVI-PoPs) and the lifecycle 218 
management of Network Services (NS). 219 
ETSI GS NFV-IFA 010 [8] specifies the principles and scope of the NFV-MANO architectural framework and 220 
functional requirements of the functional blocks and functions that are part of it.  221 
A set of groups specifications (GS) specifies the interfaces exposed and consumed by the NFV -MANO functional 222 
blocks and functions. The following are the GS of relevance for the present document:  223 
- ETSI GS NFV-IFA 007 [7] specifies stage 2 interfaces and associated information model of interfaces between 224 
the NFVO and the VNFM. ETSI GS NFV-SOL 003 [11] specifies stage 3 protocols and data models of these 225 
interfaces based on RESTful. The Release 4 version of ETSI GS NFV-SOL 003 [11] adds support for container-226 
based VNF deployments. Refer to clause 2.4.1 for the specific version and features (VM and/or OS container-227 
based deployments) supported by the present document. 228

In addition to the composition of the NFV-MANO architectural framework in terms of functional blocks, functions and 242 
interfaces, one of the key aspects is the definition of both design-time and runtime elements. Design-time elements 243 
basically comprise those objects and artifacts that describe the design and requirements for the deployment of entities 244 
into or using the framework. The design-time objects comprise packages and descriptors, among which the relevant 245 
ones for the present document are the VNF Package and VNF Descriptor (VNFD).  246 
- ETSI GS NFV-IFA 011 [12] specifies stage 2 requirements related to VNF Package and VNF metadata and the 247 
information model of the VNFD. ETSI GS NFV-SOL 001 [10] specifies stage 3 data model of the VNFD (in 248 
addition to other descriptors such as Network Service Descriptor (NSD) and Physical Network Function 249 
Descriptor (PNFD)) based on TOSCA, while ETSI GS NFV-SOL 006 [14] specifies the same elements but 250 
based on YANG. ETSI GS NFV-SOL 004 [13] specifies the data model and structure of the VNF Packages. 251 
2.3 ETSI NFV profiling for O2dms interfaces 252 
2.3.1 General 253 
The present and subsequent clauses describe specific aspects of the ETSI NFV profiling for the O2dms interfaces. In the 254 
present profiling version based on the "O2-D demarcation #1" introduced in clause 2.1, the functional capabilities of the 255 
NFVO, VNFM, VIM and CISM of the NFV-MANO architectural framework are mapped to the O-RAN framework as 256 
defined in clause 2.3.2. 257 
The O2dms ETSI NFV profile based on "O2-D demarcation #1" is based on the ETSI GS NFV-SOL 003 [11]. The 258 
ETSI GS NFV-SOL 003 [11] specifies, among others, the RESTful API of the "VNF Lifecycle Management interface", 259 
which is used as the foundation for providing an O-Cloud DMS exposed API for the lifecycle management of NF 260 
Deployments of O-RAN. Specific API correspondences are defined in clause 2.3.3. 261 
Finally, for the orchestration and management operations through the O2dms ETSI NFV profile, it is assumed that 262 
descriptors and packaging of corresponding NF Deployments, and consequently of O -RAN NF, are on-boarded and 263 
available to the SMO and O-Cloud DMS. In particular, it is expected that the descriptors and packaging for O -RAN NF 264 
contain relevant metadata such as identifiers of the design-time definitions of components/constituents of an NF 265 
Deployment, which are also information either conveyed or exposed through the API. Additional mapping information 266 
about descriptor and packaging artifacts is provided in clause 2.3.2, and reference to specific elements concerning 267 
various orchestration aspects are further described in clause 2.5. 268

Clause 3.6 of O-RAN.WG6.ORCH-USE-CASES [2] describes use cases about recovery at different level. Clause 3.6.2 637 
of O-RAN.WG6.ORCH-USE-CASES [2] details the "NF Deployment level reset use case". According to the use case, 638 
when some fatal error is identified which is expected (but not guaranteed) it can be resolved by resetting the NF 639

© 2022 by the O-RAN ALLIANCE e.V. Your use is subject to the copyright statement on the cover page of this specification. 20 
 
O-RAN.WG6.O2DMS-INTERFACE-ETSI-NFV-PROFILE-v02.00 
Deployment, the SMO can send a specific reset request to the DMS over the O2dms interface. In addition, clause 3.7.1 640 
of O-RAN.WG6.O2-GA&P [4] lists for the O-Cloud the capability of healing an O-RAN Cloudified NF instance. From 641 
the ETSI NFV profile perspective, the (part of) reset capabilities can be performed via a "Healing VNF instance" 642 
operation. 643 
As defined in ETSI GR NFV 003 [i.ref-nfv003], VNF healing is a lifecycle procedure that includes all virtualisation-644 
related corrective actions to repair a faulty VNF, and/or its VNFC instances and internal VNF Virtual Links (refer to 645 
concepts mapping in clause 2.3.2). The procedure is intended to leverage the resource virtualisation management 646 
capabilities such as, starting/stopping/restarting VMs, re-allocating VMs or containerized workloads, and other possible 647 
actions, as means to recover from faulty conditions of the VNF. Furthermore, VNF healing can be performed on-648 
demand when a consumer explicitly requests to the producer of the lifecycle management operation or automatically 649 
triggered by the producer, also referred as "auto-healing", based on various inputs such as indicators, alarms and KPIs 650 
processed from the VNF and/or infrastructure. 651 
NOTE: As described above, VNF healing considers virtualisation-related corrective actions, and it does not cover 652 
other possible VNF correction actions at the application level. 653 
The healing capabilities of a VNF are defined by the VNF provider through the VNF descriptors. The specific list of 654 
parameters to convey the trigger of what healing action to perform are also defined through VNF descriptors (see 655 
generic information modelling in clause 7.1.5.6 of ETSI GS NFV-IFA 011 [12]). Healing actions can be different 656 
between VNFs and there are no specific means specified by the referenced ETSI NFV specifications to determine what 657 
actions are applicable to a VNF instance and not to another VNF instance as this are regarded to be VNF -specific and 658

2.5.3 Network connectivity for NF Deployments 973 
Network connectivity information of NF Deployments in the O2dms ETSI NFV profile is exposed and/or conveyed via 974 
specific data types. Furthermore, in terms of network connectivity the profile modelling is based on the following key 975 
concepts: virtual links and connection points. 976 
A virtual link is an abstraction of a network, which is used by the management and orchestration functions in the ETSI 977 
NFV architectural framework. Once applied to the O-RAN framework, virtual links also become an abstraction of 978 
connectivity which is exposed and used over the O2dms interface. Virtual links can be realized in various forms such as 979 
virtual/overlay networks in an O-Cloud site, or a composition of various networks. Virtual links can be internal or 980 
external to an NF Deployment: 981 
- Internal virtual link: it expresses the connectivity between the components or constituents of an NF Deployment. 982 
- External virtual link: it expresses the connectivity external to the NF Deployment, i.e., networks that are used to 983 
interconnect among NF Deployments, or between NF Deployments and other en tities such as to other O-RAN 984 
NF, xApps, or even other systems such as management and orchestration ones.  985 
Internal virtual links can be modelled in two forms depending on the entity that manages the resources realizing the 986 
virtual link: 987 
- NF Deployment virtual link: it denotes an internal NF Deployment virtual link that is managed by the O -Cloud 988 
DMS. The data type providing information about such kind of virtual link is named 989 
"VnfVirtualLinkResourceInfo" (see clause 3.2.6.3.9). 990 
- Externally-managed virtual link: it denotes an internal NF Deployment virtual link that is managed externally to 991 
the O-Cloud DMS, e.g., in the present profiling case the SMO. The data type providing information about such 992 
kind of virtual link is named "ExtManagedVirtualLinkInfo" (see clause 3.2.6.3.7). 993
