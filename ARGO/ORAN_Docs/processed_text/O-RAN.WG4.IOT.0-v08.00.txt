1.2 Scope 14 
1.2.1  General  15 
The present document specifies the interoperability testing (IOT) for O-DU and O-RU from different vendors connected 16 
using the O-RAN fronthaul (FH) interface. It is noted however that the same content can be utilized for the IOT for O-17 
DU and O-RU from the same vendor connected using the O-RAN FH interface. 18 
A guiding principle defining the tests in this document shall be that any test must exercise the fronthaul interface to a 19 
greater extent than the test prerequisite so that the test is a proper test of som e fronthaul functionality or performance. 20 
All tests focus on testing the fronthaul interface, but due to the non -intrusive nature of the tests, system-level aspects of 21 
the O-DU and O-RU are inevitably part of the interoperability tests too. Because of this, it is recognized that positive 22 
test outcomes indicate successful interoperability, but negative results may not be attributed solely to problems in the 23 
FH interface and additional investigation may be required. 24 
It is also noted that additional test scenarios are required for comprehensive testing of the FH interface functionality and 25 
performance, which shall be considered in future WG4 test specification versions. 26 
In general, unless otherwise stated, the tests cover LTE (Stand-Alone), NR Non-Stand-Alone (NSA) and NR Stand-27 
Alone (SA). The tests shall be executed using whichever IOT profile(s) are appropriate for the DUTs. See Annex A for 28 
details on the IOT profiles. 29

1.5 Introduction 1 
1.5.1 General 2 
O-RAN WG4 has specified and published the O-RAN FH interface specifications (CUS-Plane [2] and M-Plane [3]) 3 
with the objective to enable interoperability of O-DU and O-RU from different vendors. The aim of this document is to 4 
further facilitate such multi-vendor IOT using the O-RAN FH interface by defining standard test configurations, IOT 5 
profiles and interoperability test cases. 6 
It shall be possible to perform interoperability testing in a non-intrusive manner; that is, in a manner in which the 7 
network elements under test are not required to support any functionality or mode of operation beyond that required for 8 
normal operation in a telecommunication network. However, making the endpoints of the FH interface between the O-9 
DU and O-RU visible would require definition of new interfaces which may entail caching and transport of data. 10 
Furthermore, and more importantly, operators require that the total radio system function and perform adequately when 11 
integrating O-DU and O-RU from different vendors. Consequentially, this specification approaches interoperability 12 
testing by means of system level testing. 13 
FH interoperability testing by way of system test involves creation of a stimulus in the O-DU to O-RU direction using 14 
an actual or emulated O-CU, potentially with CN support/emulation, and measurement of the result at the output of the 15 
O-RU in the RF domain by an actual or emulated UE together with an RF signal/spectrum analyzer as required. 16 
Likewise, in the reverse direction, the stimulus to probe the FH in the O-RU to O-DU direction is provided by an actual 17 
or emulated UE and is measured at the output of the O-DU by an actual or emulated O-CU with CN support/emulation 18 
as required. 19 
Inasmuch as the interoperability testing by way of system test involves configuring and collecting results from the O -20 
RU it shall be effected by means of the M-Plane FH interface. For elements that fall outside of this scope, such as the 21
